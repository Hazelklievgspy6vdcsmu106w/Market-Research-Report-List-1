<p><p>Gear driven transfer cases use a system of gears to transfer power from the vehicle's transmission to the front and rear axles. This type is known for its durability and ability to handle heavy loads. On the other hand, chain driven transfer cases use a chain to transfer power, offering smoother operation and quieter performance. These transfer cases are typically found in lighter vehicles and provide better fuel efficiency. Both types have their own advantages and are chosen based on the specific needs of the vehicle.</p></p>
