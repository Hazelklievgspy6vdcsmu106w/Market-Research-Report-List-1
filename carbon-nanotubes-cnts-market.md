<p><p>Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) are cylindrical structures made of carbon atoms that exhibit unique properties such as high strength, conductivity, and flexibility. There are two main types of CNTs: single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWCNTs) and multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWCNTs). SWCNTs consist of a single layer of carbon atoms rolled into a tube, while MWCNTs have multiple layers. These different structures lead to variations in their properties and applications, with SWCNTs being used for electronics and MWCNTs for structural reinforcement and energy storage.</p></p>
