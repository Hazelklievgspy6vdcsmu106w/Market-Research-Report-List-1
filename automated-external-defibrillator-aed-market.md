<p><p>An Automated External Defibrillator (AED) is a portable device used to deliver an electric shock to the heart in cases of sudden cardiac arrest (SCA) to restore a normal heart rhythm. It analyzes the heart's electrical activity and prompts the user to deliver a shock if necessary. AEDs are commonly found in public places such as airports, malls, and sports arenas, as they can be used by non-medical personnel, thereby increasing the chances of survival for SCA victims.</p><p>The global market for AEDs has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 5.3% during the forecast period. The growing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and the increasing awareness about the importance of immediate cardiac arrest response are the key factors driving market growth. Additionally, advancements in AED technology, such as the development of portable and user-friendly devices, have further boosted market demand.</p><p>Moreover, the rising adoption of AEDs in various public places and institutions, along with favorable government initiatives encouraging their availability and use, is also contributing to market growth. Furthermore, the increasing geriatric population, which is more susceptible to cardiac disorders, is expected to fuel the demand for AEDs.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is witnessing a shift toward the development of AEDs with enhanced connectivity and remote monitoring capabilities. These features allow medical professionals to remotely monitor the device's performance and provide immediate assistance when required. Additionally, manufacturers are focusing on the development of AEDs with improved battery life and self-diagnostic features to ensure their reliability during emergencies.</p><p>Overall, the Automated External Defibrillator (AED) Market is poised for steady growth due to increasing awareness, technological advancements, and expanding applications in public places, institutions, and healthcare settings.</p></p>

<p><p>Automated External Defibrillator (AED) is a medical device used to deliver an electric shock to the heart during cardiac arrest. There are two types of AED markets: Semi-automated and Fully automated. Semi-automated AEDs require the user to analyze the heart rhythm and manually trigger the shock if required. On the other hand, Fully automated AEDs analyze the heart rhythm themselves and automatically deliver the shock without any user intervention. Both types are essential in emergency situations, offering efficient and prompt treatment for patients experiencing cardiac arrest.</p></p>
