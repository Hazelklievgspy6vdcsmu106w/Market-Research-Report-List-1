<p><p>Olefinic Thermoplastic Elastomers are a type of polymer material known for their rubber-like elasticity and thermoplastic properties. There are two main types in the market: Thermoplastic Polyolefins (TPO) and Thermoplastic Vulcanizates (TPVs). TPOs are blends of polypropylene and elastomers, offering a balance of flexibility and durability. TPVs, on the other hand, are cross-linked blends of polyolefins and rubber, providing enhanced toughness and heat resistance. These materials are commonly used in automotive, construction, and industrial applications.</p></p>
