Drum brakes consist of brake shoes that press against the interior of a rotating drum to slow down the vehicle. Disc brakes use calipers to squeeze brake pads against a rotating disc to stop the vehicle. The other market includes emerging technologies such as regenerative braking systems and electro-hydraulic brakes. Each type offers unique advantages in terms of performance, durability, and efficiency in automotive braking systems.</p></p>
