<p><p>Dry film lubricants are a type of lubricant that is applied as a thin layer on surfaces to reduce friction and wear. There are two main types of dry film lubricants on the market: PTFE-based and Molybdenum disulfide-based. </p><p>PTFE-based dry film lubricants utilize polytetrafluoroethylene, commonly known as Teflon, to provide excellent lubrication and low friction properties. </p><p>Molybdenum disulfide-based dry film lubricants use molybdenum disulfide particles to reduce friction and provide long-lasting lubrication in high-temperature and extreme pressure conditions. These lubricants are commonly used in automotive and industrial applications.</p></p>
