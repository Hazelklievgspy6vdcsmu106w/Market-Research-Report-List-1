<p><p>MRD minimal residual disease testing is used to detect and monitor small amounts of leukemic cells remaining in a patient's body after treatment. The three main types of MRD testing are Flow Cytometry, Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR), and Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS). Flow cytometry analyzes cells based on their physical and chemical characteristics, PCR amplifies and detects specific DNA sequences, and NGS sequences all DNA in a sample to identify mutations associated with leukemia. Each technique has its own advantages and applications in the MRD testing market.</p></p>
