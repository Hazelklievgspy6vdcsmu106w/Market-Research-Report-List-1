<p><p>Automotive hydraulic power steering systems are categorised into two main types: Pressure Hydraulic Power and Constant Flow Hydraulic Power. Pressure Hydraulic Power systems rely on the pressure generated by a hydraulic pump to assist with steering, while Constant Flow Hydraulic Power systems use a constant flow of hydraulic fluid to provide steering assistance. Both systems work by helping reduce the effort required to turn the steering wheel, making it easier for drivers to maneuver their vehicles. These systems are crucial for enhancing driving comfort and control.</p></p>
