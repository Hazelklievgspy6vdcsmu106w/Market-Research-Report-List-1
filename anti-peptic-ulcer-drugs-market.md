<p><p>The anti-peptic ulcer drugs market includes various types of medications used to treat ulcers in the stomach and intestines. Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) are commonly prescribed drugs that reduce the amount of acid produced by the stomach. Potassium-Competitive Acid Blockers (P-CAB) are a newer class of drugs that work differently but have similar effects to PPIs. Antacids are over-the-counter medications that provide temporary relief by neutralizing stomach acid. H2 antagonists also reduce stomach acid but work differently from PPIs. Lastly, ulcer protective drugs help protect the lining of the stomach and promote healing.</p></p>
