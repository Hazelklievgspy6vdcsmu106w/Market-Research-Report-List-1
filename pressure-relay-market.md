<p><p>Pressure relays are devices used to control and regulate pressure within a system. There are several types of pressure relays including plunger, diaphragm, spring tube, and bellows. Plunger pressure relays use a simple plunger mechanism to control pressure, while diaphragm relays rely on a flexible diaphragm to sense pressure changes. Spring tube pressure relays utilize a coiled tube to respond to pressure signals, and bellows pressure relays use a metal bellows to measure pressure. Each type has its own unique design and applications in various industries.</p></p>
