<p><p>Liquid paraffin is commonly used in cosmetics for its moisturizing and emollient properties. This versatile ingredient can be divided into two main market types: paraffin-based and cycloalkyl-based. Paraffin-based liquid paraffin is derived from petroleum and is known for its ability to create a protective barrier on the skin. On the other hand, cycloalkyl-based liquid paraffin is made from cyclic alkanes and is favored for its lighter texture and fast-absorbing qualities. Both types offer a range of benefits for skin and hair care products.</p></p>
