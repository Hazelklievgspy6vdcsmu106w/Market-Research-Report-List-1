<p><p>Bioresorbable medical polymers are a type of material that can be absorbed by the body, eliminating the need for surgical removal after use. The market is categorized into different types such as Polylactic Acid (PLA), Polyglycolic Acid (PGA), Polysaccharides, Polycaprolactone (PCL), and PLGA. PLA and PGA are commonly used due to their biocompatibility and mechanical properties. Polysaccharides are natural polymers derived from plants or animals. PCL is known for its slow degradation rate, making it suitable for long-term applications. PLGA is a copolymer of PLA and PGA, offering a balance of properties.</p></p>
